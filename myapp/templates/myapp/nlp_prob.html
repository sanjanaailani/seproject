1. What is Probability?

Probability measures the chance of something happening. It ranges from 0 (won‚Äôt happen) to 1 (guaranteed to happen).

Formula: P(Event) = Number of favorable outcomes / Total possible outcomes

For example: If there are 2 positive reviews out of 5 total reviews, the probability that a randomly selected review is positive is:

P(positive) = 2 / 5 = 0.4

2. Key Terms in Probability

  • Experiment: A repeatable process with outcomes (e.g., picking a word from a sentence)
  • Outcome: A single possible result (e.g., selecting the word "AI")
  • Event: One or more outcomes (e.g., picking a word longer than 3 characters)
  • Sample Space: The set of all possible outcomes (e.g., all words in a corpus)
  • Random Variable: A variable whose outcome depends on chance (e.g., POS tag of a word)

3. Conditional Probability

Conditional probability measures the likelihood of an event happening given that another event has occurred.

P(A | B) = Probability of A happening given B has already happened

Example in NLP: What is the chance the word ‚Äúcold‚Äù appears after ‚Äúcatch‚Äù?

P("cold" | "catch") = Count("catch cold") / Count("catch")

This is used in language models to predict the next word!

4. Applications of Probability in NLP

  • Spam Detection: Using probability to classify emails as spam or not
  • Autocomplete & Autocorrect: Predicting likely next words or correcting misspelled ones
  • Speech Recognition: Choosing the most probable interpretation of a spoken sentence
  • Machine Translation: Picking the most probable translation of a sentence
  • Language Modeling: Calculating the probability of word sequences (e.g., in GPT and BERT)
Fun Fact: Many machine learning models (like Naive Bayes) are built entirely around probability math!

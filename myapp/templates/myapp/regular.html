Think of regex as:

  • A metal detector for patterns.
  • A search engine inside your code.
  • A way to say “find all words ending in ing” — without writing 100 if conditions.

4. Visual Example: Email Hunter

import re text = "My email is jane.doe@gmail.com and hers is abc_123@yahoo.co.in"
emails = re.findall(r'\b[\w.-]+@[\w.-]+\.\w+\b', text)
print(emails)

Output: "['jane.doe@gmail.com', 'abc_123@yahoo.co.in']"

Why it works:

  • The regex [\w.-]+@[\w.-]+\.\w+ means:

Match one or more word/dot/dash → @ → one or more word/dot → dot → word characters

5.Python Demo

Quick Quiz

Which regex will match all words that end with 'ing'?

  • A. \bing\b
  • B. \w+ing
  • C. ing\w+
  • D. [ing]

Answer: B. \w+ing

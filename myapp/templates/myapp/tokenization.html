Tokenization

1. What is a Tokenization?

Tokenization is the first step in NLP—breaking text into smaller pieces called tokens.

These tokens can be:

  • Words
  • Characters
  • Subwords (like BPE or WordPiece)
  • Or even emoji's

Example:

"I'm learning NLP!" → ["I", "'m", "learning", "NLP", "!"]


2.Why Do We Tokenize?

Because computers don’t speak text—they speak numbers.

Tokenization helps us:

  • Clean and organize text
  • Prepare it for ML models
  • Convert words into embeddings
  • Handle unknown or rare words

4.Subword Tokenization Deep Dive

Welcome to the big leagues: BPE and WordPiece

Byte Pair Encoding (BPE):

Example:["un", "happiness"]

  • →If happi and ness are frequent, they get merged.
  • Learns frequent pairs like:
    • h + a = ha
    • ha + p = hap
    • hap + p = happ
  • Used in models like GPT, OpenAI CLIP

WordPiece:

Example: "undeniably" → ["un", "den", "i", "ably"]

  • Keeps known words whole
  • Breaks unknown ones into parts

Used in:

  • BERT
  • ALBERT
  • DistilBERT
